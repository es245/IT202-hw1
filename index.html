Tourism

Where exactly is Ohrid?

Ohrid is located in Southeastern Europe in what is known as the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the country of Macedonia and closely borders Albania and some of Greece. This particular area provides a warm climate for hot summers and sunshine throughout most of it's peak tourism season. Ohrid is a host of several tourist attractions. Aside from the beautiful weather and culture, Ohrid provides:

  • Beaches
  • Historical Monuments and Museums
  • Churches
  • an Amphitheatre
  • Cafes and Nightclubs

Beaches

Looking for a perfect place to unwind, take a swim, and get a nice tan? Look no further, as Ohrid has over 50 beaches running alonside the coast of beautiful Lake Ohrid. The lake can accomadate fun for almost any occassion as it provides amenities such as cafes, bars, playgrounds, jet skis, and paddleboats all conveniently at the coast. Not to mention the countless nightclubs that take full advantage of the georgous view of the lake and are open late at night right on top of the water.

Churches

Historically, Macedonia has been known as a religious country, however, Ohrid has over 365 churches within it's walls alone. Each and every one houses its own traditional architecture and usually is named after the many saints in the Christian Orthodox faith. The most famous, the one you see pictured up top, is named after Saint Jovan at Kaneo, and is a huge tourist attraction, as the climb up top to Kaneo is full of shops, cobblestone paths, ancient houses, and cafes along the way.

History

Ohrid is known for a plethora of historical sites and museums. Scattered all over the city are monuments, ancient buildings,tours, a castle, and the historic Amphitheatre which is used to this day for musical performances. Tourists seeking fun will definitely find it, but those history buffs and architecture junkies will have hundreds of locations to discover and admire alongside the beautiful coast and cobblestone path that cuts through the heart of Ohrid known as the Чаршија (Char-shi-ya).

History

Not only does Ohrid claim to be an almost perfect tourist destination, summer escape, and natural wonder, but it also has much to learn from its walls as well.

  • Ohrid is home to one of Europe's oldest lakes, Lake Ohrid. It has also been declared a world heritage site by UNESCO, given its vast biosphere and age.
    • Lake Ohrid has been dated to be up to 5 million years old.
    • It is also one of the deepest lakes in the Balkan Region.
    • It's home to over 200 species of marine and land species.
  • There are over 350 churches, each with its own unique construct and dedication to a patron saint of the orthodox religion, the most famous church being located on top of a mountain called Kaneo for Saint Jovan.
  • Near the top of Kaneo, and overlooking the whole lake, stands an ancient fortress that belong to the Tsar Samuel in the 4th centery B.C. There are tours that allow people inside to the fortress along with the church of St. Jovan.
  • There are plenty of other historical sites, monuments, museums, and buildings to explore, and tourists will find many guest service kiosks and centers to help them decide on which ones to induldge in.
